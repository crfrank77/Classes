# MAE 156 A & 156B Fundamental Principles of Mechanical Design I & II

MAE 156 A & B serve as the capstone course series for the Mechanical Engineering Program at UCSD. The courses focus on the fundamental principles of mechanical design and the design process along with the application of engineering science to the design and analysis of mechanical components. STudents work in groups of 5 to complete an engineering project for an outside group (i.e. an on campus lab, research group, company, or individual)

# Carbon Blade Project 
My project was a collaboration with the San Diego start-up Carbon Blade. The Passive Direct Air Capture (DAC) project, designed, fabricated, and tested a blade system for atmospheric carbon dioxide removal. This device would work as a part of Carbon Blades carbon capture system called CAPTUS, which was intended to be a small modular CO2 capture system with easy deployment capabilities. The device worked passively, utilizing local wind and low power demand electrical components powered by on board renewable energy sources. The final presentations and summaries from this project are contained in this folder. My work on this project resulted in Carbon Blade filing a patent where I hold specific claims on an invention disclosure supporting the filing.
